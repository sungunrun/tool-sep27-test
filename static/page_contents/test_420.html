The foregoing is a highly schematized sketch of brain circuits underlying visual pro-
cessing. Nearly all brain areas are connected to nearly all others. All or nearly all these
connections are reciprocal, yielding constant feedback as well as feed-forward processing.
All the mentioned areas subdivide into sub-areas. So the information traffic map is vastly
more complex and multi-routed than the sketch suggests. Brain areas cited as specializing
in supporting certain perceptual functions always have other perception-supporting sub-
specializations that grow more prominent in some circumstances via effects of attention.
A given type of specialized processing commonly occurs in many areas. The dorsal and
ventral streams are connected from the outset of their divergence at V1. Strong inter-
stream connections are in place from V2 onwards. Inter-connections among V3, the
temporal area, and the pre-frontal area are examples.

Feedback routes accompany feed-forward routes. Feedback is initiated from both
adjacent and distant areas. Because of ubiquitous interconnection, no area is entirely
encapsulated or single-purpose. The sketch is meant to frame further discussion—espe-
cially of timing, perceptual constancies, and connections among perception and satellite
capacities, such as memory.

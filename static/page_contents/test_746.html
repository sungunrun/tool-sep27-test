Almost surely, some representation is cognitive, but not propositional (Chapter 19, the
section What Should Count as Cognition?). However, in the evolution of repre-
sentation beyond the perceptual level, the emergence of propositional forms is the most
momentous. This emergence provided the basis for explaining and understanding, not
merely tracking, the world. Propositional forms can articulate why-questions and because-
answers. They make possible reasons and reasoning, in full, unattenuated senses of these
terms. They make possible science, understanding, morality, and the deeper sorts of art—
enterprises that make representation a substantial contributor to goods of life.

Central to understanding the emergence of propositional form is understanding how
attribution took on other functions besides its one function in perceptual-level represen-
tation, that of guiding referential application. Being freed from always guiding referential
application enabled attribution to dominate a representational structure. Such domination
marks propositional structure. (See Chapter 6, the section Absence in Perception
of Negations, Conditionals, Disjunctions, and Quantifiers; Chapter 19,
the section Psychological Systems and Psychological Kinds.)1209

Taking on new functions, of course, depends on acquisition of new representational
capacities. So understanding what is distinctive of propositional structures hinges on
understanding the attributive capacities that pushed beyond guiding referential applica-
tion. These new capacities made rational enterprises possible. Such enterprises peaked in
science–and understanding of other sorts—and in morality.

Perception: The Seed of New Things to Live and Die For

Perception emerged in evolution as an aid to fitness for reproduction. It earned its keep not
primarily by realizing its own distinctive representational functions, but by serving bio-
logical functions. Still, causal patterns and causal laws incorporated a new kind of state–a
state typed by the accuracy conditions that it sets. Given that perceptual representation
sets accuracy conditions and undergoes a type of failure if those accuracy conditions are
not met, perceptual states have a new function—being accurate. Success in being accurate
is a new type of success.

Grounding this new state-kind and this new function was a new capacity, a capacity to
represent. Representation required capacities to mine registration of proximal stimulation
for cues to the environment. Objectifying capacities to isolate cues to perception-independent
conditions in the idiosyncracies of proximal stimulation are perceptual constancies. The
states’ accuracy conditions center on distal matters.

The representational point of perception is to pick out and track distal particulars via
causal sensory relations to those particulars. So perception must pick out particulars rep-
resentationally. This point serves biological needs because particulars within causal reach
are what an organism must engage with successfully in order to eat, mate, and stay safe.

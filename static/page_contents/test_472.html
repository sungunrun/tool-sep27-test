Much of the structure of infant primitive agency-representation occurs in other higher
mammals and in birds. Details of anticipations and sensitivities vary with species, or
groups of individuals in a species.

As mentioned earlier, early attributions of agency and animacy are closely connected. I
think that agency representation is developmentally earlier than animacy representation.
Possibly, at some developmental stage, a representation indicates equally animals and
agents without distinguishing them. They do tend to occur together. I think it more likely
that agency attribution comes first. It may be present from the start. In any case, once
agential structure emerges in representation—act/target, act/target-object structure, for
example—agency is attributed.

Much of the literature on developmental psychology runs together the two types of
attributives. Relevant cues for primitive attributions of animacy are said to be facial body-
parts, eye body-parts, and certain types of texture and contour. There is some evidence
that infants treat some (but not all) non-human species (with faces and eyes) differently
from other objects, at least with some exposure. It is not clear that these cues are not cues
at first only to attributions of agency. The same can be said about various motion cues that
are plausibly triggers of agency attribution.

By 4 to 6 years of age, children are fairly good at distinguishing living from non-living
objects in their reports. They tend to associate life with animacy (largely ignoring or exclud-
ing plants). In this same rough period and up to 10 years of age, children tend to base their
reports that something is alive on observations of target-directed agency.628 Attributions of
animacy are also associated, at around 4 years of age, with biological processes—growth, heal-
ing, reproduction, inheritance, illness, contagion.629 Relatively clear attribution of an animate-
inanimate distinction is present by 2–3 years of age. Again, I think that agency is probably a
fundamental cue for animacy attribution, suggesting that agency attribution comes first.630

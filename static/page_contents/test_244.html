Accuracy Conditions for Perceptual Attributives in Perceptual Constancies

An accuracy condition for a perceptual representational content is stated simply:

An instance of a perceptual state is accurate if and only if: all referential applications in the
state’s representational content (including any that fix an egocentric anchor for a spatial or
temporal framework) refer; all attributives indicate an attribute; and all referred-to par-
ticulars have all attributes attributed to them.

I believe that such a gloss is complete. I discuss a temptation to go further.

Imagine three drawings. Each is to represent, as accurately as possible, the scene with
the brown surface that confronts the artist. In the first, a brown surface is presented in
white light; the drawing is fully accurate of the specific shade of brown. In the second, the
color-shade occurs in red light; the drawing exactly matches the first drawing. In the third,
the color-shade occurs in red light; the drawing represents the brown surface as brown,
and in a way appropriate to its being red-lit. Although the second drawing draws the
brownness of the patch, there is a sense in which it is not fully accurate. It is inaccurate
even though it draws the color shade that the surface in fact has.

brown color-shade requires representing it in a way appropriate to its being lit as it is in
fact lit. Representational contents set veridicality conditions. So, on this view, the represen-

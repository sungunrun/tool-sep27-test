The basic problem for the reasoning just discussed is a defective conception of what a
concept is. The main mark of the conceptual is contributing constitutively to supra-
perceptual-level capacities, prominently propositional inference. The main mark of what it
is to be a perceptual attributive is being generated by a perceptual system, comprised of
perceptual or perceptual-level competencies. We learn what a perceptual system is em pir-
ic al ly, as a relevantly unified system of perceptual or perceptual-level capacities. We learn
what perceptual and perceptual-level capacities are by starting with defeasible, modifiable
conceptions and refining them via empirical investigation. It is empirically known, I think,
that perceptual systems generate only attributives that do not function constitutively to
contribute to propositional capacities.

Richer Perceptual Categorization and Perceptual

Processing that Contributes to It

I proceed on the assumption, grounded in perceptual psychology, that there are perceptual
attributives that categorize. Categorizations of bodies and faces by 3-D volume shape
are cases.

The main drift of the earliest stages of visual processing is from coarse, low-spatial-
frequency representation to finer-grained representation with higher spatial frequency.
The time course of perceptual categorization proceeds from super-super-ordinate and
super-ordinate categorization to more specific—basic level, subordinate, and recognition-
level—categorization. From the outset, there is recurrent feedback in and among visual
areas.655 But during the first 120–150 ms after stimulus onset, perceptual processing is
dominantly feedforward. Such processing produces, by 60–80 ms, categorizations. First-
formed cat egor iza tions are embedded in spatial constancies, which usually derive from
application bases in spatial property attributives. A primate visual perceptual system can
attribute face, body, and animal-body under a wide range of proximal stimulations in this
first pass. (See Chapter 10.)

The primary spatial constancies are those of location, size, surface shape, and 3-D vol-
ume shape. This is “at a glance” perception. Despite its sketch-like character, it can be the
most crucial type for preservation of life. Processing grows more specific in the next 70–80
ms, to 150 ms, as higher spatial-frequency information is integrated into the attributives.656

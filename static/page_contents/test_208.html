In this chapter, I refine the form and semantics of perceptual attributives and referential
applications. A focus on perceptual constancies contributes to understanding these
matters.

Perceptual Constancies and Frege’s Sense–Bedeutung

Distinction: Similarities

In Origins of Objectivity, I claimed that we could improve understanding of perceptual
constancies by reflecting on analogies to the informative identity thoughts that Frege used
to illuminate the senses of linguistic expressions and the conceptual contents of thought.201
I pursue this idea here. I review Frege’s point. Then I explain its analogs in perceptual
constancies. I develop these analogies bearing in mind differences between perception
and thought.

Frege contrasted two kinds of true identity statements. One has the form a = a. The
other has the form a = b.202 ‘=’ reads: is identical with. In the first kind, the same singular
term occurs on each side of the identity sign. In the second, different singular terms occur
on each side. Frege said that the two types of statement have different “cognitive values”.
He meant by ‘different cognitive values’ different types of ideal understanding with differ-
ent potentials for cognition.

Frege illustrated his point with a pair of specific identity statements:

(a-a) The Morning Star is identical with the Morning Star

(a-b) The Morning Star is identical with the Evening Star.

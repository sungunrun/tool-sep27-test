On all these theories, operations in imagining and perceiving are parallel. The timing
results suggest that similar operations work at different time scales, and different levels of
endogenous control. Neither rotation, nor comparison by a similarity metric to a tem-
plate, nor shape-determination paced, temporally, by degree of statistics-based confidence
is a supra-perceptual operation. Operations in perceptual imagining appear to be similar
to operations in perception. No wonder. The contents of imagining are derived from per-
ception. It is not surprising that the operations also derive from perception. Shepard him-
self regarded mental rotation as a relatively straightforward extension of perceptual
capacities, not a “higher” capacity.1043

As with some types of short-term memory, formation and control of images are sup-
ported in brain areas that overlap areas that support vision. This point is less important
than the points about form, format, type of processing, and content, in determining
whether relevant imagining is perceptual-level or supra-perceptual. The neural evidence
is, however, congenial with the idea that visual imagining is not a type of representation on
the other side from perception of a deep divide among psychological kinds.1044 Of course,
imagining is a genus with perceptual-level and supra-perceptual level species. However,
imagining per se is not a form of cognition.

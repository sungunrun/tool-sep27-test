The progression in development and learning is certainly a significant driver in the
development of cognitive capacities.1186 Individuals become more consciously sensitive to
more generic structural relations as they develop and become more educated. Analogizing
is an important domain for reflecting on cognition and its differences from perceptual-
level capacities.

Our issue is where the progression begins. Do analogical operations already occur in
perceptual systems? Or are such operations always cognitive?

Functional relations like mate-of and gives-to may well be perceptually attributed. Mere
attribution of such relations is not analogizing. Even if such relations are perceptually
attributed, analogizing may not occur at perceptual levels. For an analogical operation in a
perceptual-level system would be a transition from representing relatively concrete rela-
tions among perceived entities—say, spatial relations—to representing different types of
relations, such as agential or functional relations, that the particulars also enter into.
Analogical operations use attributions of more concrete relations to trigger formation of
more generic relations. The fact that both spatial relations and agential, or other functional
relations, are represented in a perceptual system would not entail that there are analogiz-
ing transitions from the former to the latter in the system.

Similar issues arise among unquestionably perceptual relational attributives. Relations
like larger than and farther from, and symmetry relations are certainly attributed per cep-
tual ly. Is a move from attributing these relations to focused-upon particulars to attending
to a common relation among different particulars analogizing?

The classical signature of analogical operations is comparison leading to relational
alignment. Comparison is a domain-general process that often leads to detection of com-
monalities or differences in a pair of situations or domains. Alignment involves one–one
mapping among relata such that if a pair of relational attributives are placed in cor res-
pond ence, then the representations that are related are placed in correspondence.1187 One
needs to determine whether this signature shows up in operations in perceptual systems.

Can comparison and alignment be applied, perhaps only unconsciously, at the percep-
tual level to yield analogies? For example, can individuals, relying on perception and per-
ceptual learning alone, use pair-wise size comparisons of size to trigger focus on a generic
relation like smaller than? Can seeing different individuals in different spatial relations
that are in fact giving something to other individuals trigger comparison and alignment
among the individuals so as to attribute a giving, w, of x, by y, to z? If perceptual attribu-
tives for action are acquired by perceptual learning, it is plausible that they can be acquired
by comparison and alignment.

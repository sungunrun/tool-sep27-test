A further study tries to home in on action attribution. Lower-level motion-perception
areas, such as MT+ (an amalgam of the middle temporal area, MT, and the medial su per-
ior temporal area, MST) are reported to underlie responses to the motion and shape orien-
ta tion involved in chasing.634 Specific types of motion and shape orientation, associated
with attribution of chasing and threatening, seem to be grounded in uncontroversially
visual perceptual areas of the brain.

The basic issue remains. Perceptual attributions of distinctive motion-and shape orien-
ta tion cue agency attribution. Such attributions are surely formed because of their connec-
tion to agency, but are not agency representations. Functional dispositions to respond to
these cues are central. The dispositions are essential to differentiating agency representa-
tion from motion/spatial-relation/bodily-shape representation. Are the dispositions
perceptual or supra-perceptual?

As far as I know, neurophysiological investigation of these functional responses has not
been undertaken.635 More studies tracking more factors in agency attribution would be
valuable in supporting or disconfirming the view that primitive attributions of agency are
perceptual.

Attribution of biological motion and other types of joint relations between different
bodies is certainly perceptual. Since such motions are grouped as special largely because
they signal agency, it is plausible that when supported by functional anticipations, attribu-
tion of biological motion and relevant spatial configurations triggers perceptual attribu-
tions of agency.636

In the initial experiments, the photographs were rather different from one another.
Many psychologists proposed that only their gist was remembered. Later studies showed,
however, that substantial detail is retained.946 Scenes are not remembered with photo-
graphic detail. But a lot of detail, especially regarding salient, interesting aspects of a scene,
such as a bedroom and many of its objects in their spatial relations, are retained. Modern
science of long-term visual memory takes relevant memories to be stored in iconic form.
There is also no essential appeal to propositional capacities in accounts of the mechanisms
of retention or the nature of the iconic representations. Experiments track perceptual
VLTM, though VLTM also retains propositional/conceptual contents. Humans can express
what they remember in conceptual, indeed linguistic, form.

Extrapolation from similar cases has led to a view in the science that there is no evident
upper bound on the capacity to store information in visual long-term memory. Long-term
memory is more obviously limited by relatively small differences among entities to be
remembered (roughly, crowding) than by amount of visual information. Retention of per-
ceptual representation in visual long-term memory can last a life-time.

Several factors enter into forgetting—normal decay and various types of interference.
Details of particular events or scenes are lost before more general, salient aspects are.947 In
some forgetting, memory traces are lost. In other cases, access to memories becomes diffi-
cult. Memory researchers use subtle methods to distinguish these cases.948

In this chapter I discuss relations between perceptual reference and perceptual attribution.

Basic Form Of Perceptual Contents

The basic form of perceptual representational contents, broached in Chapter 2, is

(that x1)F(x1).

All perception contains in its representational content at least one occurrence of a scope-
dominant context-dependent referential determiner-type, analogous to the demonstrative
‘that’ (or ‘those’), conceived as a word-type. Normally, there are multitudes of referential
applications of determiners in a perceptual state. The determiner-type is a repeatable com-
petence for singular reference.

The determiner is associated with at least one perceptual attributive, analogous to F . F is a
representational content. The content is a repeatable attributional competence. Normally, there
are multitudes of such attributives. Always, some attributive guides referential application of a
determiner. Indeed, every occurrence of a determiner applies at least one guiding attributive.137

A referential application, marked by the subscript ‘1’, is an occurrent application of the
determiner. It is both an exercise of the referential competence, typed by that, and an
application of one or more attributives. The exercise/application is an occurrent event, or
occurrent aspect of a state. The referential application both functions to refer to a per-
ceived particular and initiates application of the attributives(s)—initiates attributional
applications. Attributional applications function to characterize perceived entities picked
out by the referential applications.
